## *Reflection (300–500 words) addressing:*
• What did you choose to track, and why?

The aspect I chose about my daily life is the colours of objects that I physically interact with. This will be any physical I may touch with my two hands. I am interested in how colourful or lack of colour there is with my everyday items. I believe that choosing this would provide myself with a large yet interesting look into the colour data of my daily life. Though I may care about colours when it comes to purchasing a product or choosing what to wear, I have never kept track of this detail.


• What was it like to collect and visualise this data?

When to came to collecting my data, it was simple yet mildly frustrating. In the day to day, I would interact with a lot of objects. I knew the colours of my essential items that I would often interact with. For example my phone, tooth brush, and clothes. I would also have to some more fun items like any fingers foods/ingredients when making a meal. However I do think my data was not anything too wild as it was collected during a normal and slightly repetitive university week.


• What did you notice that you wouldn't have otherwise?


• What choices did you make for your data collection? What does it emphasise? What is left out?

The choices I made during the collection of my data was a mix of comprimises. Although I did not write too often within my physical notebook, I did keep a mental note for most of time. When I reached my notebook, I put down all my main details for the day. However this system did mean I would miss some data points when noting. My use of a physical notepad was a good change from a digital system as it made my journey feel a bit more purposeful. Digital writing does not equal actual even though it may be more efficient.
